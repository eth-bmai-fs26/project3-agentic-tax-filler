From Quellensteuer to Ordinary Filing: A Step-by-Step Guide

10. Januar 2026Lesezeit: 5 Min.QuellensteuerC-BewilligungAusländer

You just received your C permit — congratulations! But now you need to switch from Quellensteuer (source tax) to ordinary tax filing. This transition confuses almost everyone. Here is exactly what to do.

What Changes with the C Permit?

Under Quellensteuer, your employer deducted tax directly from your salary each month. With ordinary taxation, you now file a full Steuererklärung and pay taxes based on the final assessment. The employer stops deducting Quellensteuer.

The Full-Year Rule

This is the part that surprises people: you must declare your full-year income, not just the months after the transition.

Critical: If you received your C permit in October, you declare income from January through December — the entire year. The Quellensteuer already deducted from January through September is entered as a tax credit, not ignored.

Step-by-Step in ZHprivateTax

  1. Income section: Enter your full-year gross salary from the Lohnausweis (which covers all 12 months)
  2. Quellensteuer credit: Find the total Quellensteuer deducted on your Lohnausweis (or on your monthly payslips). Enter this amount in the designated field for already-paid taxes
  3. All deductions: Claim all deductions you are entitled to for the full year — commuting, meals, Pillar 3a, etc.
  4. Final bill: The tax office calculates your total tax for the year, subtracts the Quellensteuer already paid, and you pay (or get refunded) the difference

Common Mistakes

  • Declaring only post-transition income: Wrong — declare the full year
  • Forgetting the Quellensteuer credit: This means you'd be taxed twice on the pre-transition months
  • Not claiming deductions for the full year: You are entitled to full-year deductions (Pillar 3a, commuting, etc.) even though you were on Quellensteuer for part of the year
Tip: The transition year often results in a refund, because Quellensteuer is typically calculated at a flat rate without considering personal deductions. Once you file ordinarily and claim all your deductions, the effective tax rate is often lower.

What About B Permit Holders?

B permit holders generally remain on Quellensteuer. However, if annual gross income exceeds CHF 120,000 (in Zurich), they must file an ordinary Steuererklärung alongside the Quellensteuer. This is called the "nachträgliche ordentliche Veranlagung." The rules and procedures differ slightly — check the current cantonal guidelines.
