The Bicycle Deduction Nobody Knows About

8. Januar 2026Lesezeit: 5 Min.FahrkostenVeloPendeln

There is a CHF 700 tax deduction hiding in plain sight on the Zurich Steuererklärung, and almost nobody claims it — because they think you need to own a bicycle. You do not.

What Is the Velo-Pauschale?

The Velo-Pauschale is a flat-rate deduction of CHF 700 per year for commuting costs. It appears in the Fahrkosten section of the Steuererklärung alongside public transport and car commuting deductions.

The Rule Most People Get Wrong

The Zurich Steuerbuch states that the Velo-Pauschale can be claimed as a base deduction for qualifying commute distances. Crucially, it does not require the taxpayer to actually own or ride a bicycle. The deduction is distance-based, not vehicle-based.

The surprising rule: The CHF 700 Velo-Pauschale in Zurich can be claimed for commute distances that fall within the qualifying range, regardless of whether you own a bicycle, have ever ridden one, or intend to. It functions as a base commuting allowance.

Who Qualifies?

The qualification is based on commute distance:

  • Your one-way commute must be within the distance range that the Steuerbuch defines as reasonable for bicycle commuting
  • Generally, this means commutes of approximately 5–15 km one way
  • For very short commutes (under ~1–2 km), the deduction may not apply
  • For very long commutes (over ~15–20 km), the public transport deduction is typically more relevant

Can You Combine It with Public Transport?

In many cases, yes. If your commute involves both a short cycling-distance segment and a longer public transport segment, you may be able to claim both. For example, if you take the tram for the main route and could theoretically cycle to the tram stop, the combination may be valid. However, the total commuting deduction is always subject to the Zurich cantonal cap.

Example
Stefan commutes 8 km by tram from his apartment in Zurich-Wiedikon to his office in Zurich-Oerlikon. He does not own a bicycle. His ZVV annual pass costs CHF 2,840. He can claim:

• ZVV annual pass: CHF 2,840
• Velo-Pauschale: CHF 700 (his 8 km distance qualifies)
• Total: CHF 3,540 (subject to cantonal cap)

Without knowing about the Velo-Pauschale, Stefan would have claimed only CHF 2,840 — leaving CHF 700 on the table.
Important: Always check the current year's Zurich Steuerbuch for the exact distance thresholds, as they can be updated. The CHF 700 amount and qualification criteria should be verified against the latest guidance.
