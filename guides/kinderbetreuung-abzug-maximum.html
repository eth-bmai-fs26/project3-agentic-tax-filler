Childcare Costs: The Cap Nobody Reads About

14. Februar 2026Lesezeit: 5 Min.KinderbetreuungKiTaAbzüge

You spent CHF 18,000 on daycare this year. You carefully saved the receipt. You enter CHF 18,000 in your Steuererklärung. And then the tax office corrects it down to CHF 10,100. What happened?

The Zurich Cantonal Cap

The Canton of Zurich caps the Kinderbetreuungskosten deduction at CHF 10,100 per child per year for Kantonssteuer purposes. No matter how much you actually spend, the maximum deduction is CHF 10,100.

The cap: Zurich Kinderbetreuungskosten maximum = CHF 10,100 per child for Kantonssteuer. The federal cap may differ — check the current year's Wegleitung for the exact federal maximum.

What Qualifies as Kinderbetreuungskosten?

  • KiTa / Krippe (daycare): Yes — the most common claim
  • Hort / Tagesschule (after-school care): Yes
  • Tagesmutter (day nanny / childminder): Yes, if officially registered
  • Au-pair with childcare duties: Potentially, proportional to childcare hours
  • Babysitting by grandparents: No — unless they are formally employed and paid (rare)
  • Summer camps with childcare component: Potentially, if the primary purpose is care (not education or sport)

Age Limit

The Kinderbetreuungskosten deduction applies only for children under 14 years old. Once the child turns 14, the deduction drops away — even if after-school care continues.

Watch the ages: Sofia (age 7) qualifies for childcare deductions if she attends a Hort. But you need evidence — the KiTa receipt alone doesn't cover a school-age child. For school-age children, you need a separate Hort or Tagesschule receipt.

Kinderbetreuung vs. Kinderabzug

Don't confuse these:

DeductionWhat It IsAge Limit
KinderabzugGeneral child deduction (per child)Under 18 (or under 25 if in education)
KinderbetreuungskostenActual childcare costsUnder 14

You can claim both for the same child — they are separate deductions.
