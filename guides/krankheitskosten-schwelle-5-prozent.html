Medical Expenses and Taxes: The 5% Threshold That Changes Everything

26. Februar 2026Lesezeit: 5 Min.KrankheitskostenAbzügeGesundheit

You spent CHF 5,200 on dental work, prescriptions, and physiotherapy this year. You dutifully collected all receipts. But when the tax assessment comes back, the deduction is CHF 0. Why? Because of the 5% threshold.

The Rule

In the Canton of Zurich, medical expenses (Krankheits- und Unfallkosten) are deductible only to the extent they exceed 5% of your net income.

The formula:
Deductible amount = Total medical costs − (5% × net income)

If the result is negative or zero → no deduction available.

Worked Example

Example: Thomas & Elisabeth
Total medical costs: CHF 5,200 (dentist CHF 2,800 + pharmacy CHF 900 + physiotherapy CHF 1,500)
Combined net income: ~CHF 156,000
5% threshold: CHF 7,800

CHF 5,200 − CHF 7,800 = −CHF 2,600

Result: CHF 0 deduction. The medical costs are entirely below the threshold.

When Medical Expenses ARE Deductible

The deduction becomes meaningful in two scenarios:

  • High medical costs: A major procedure (surgery, dental implants, extended hospital stay) that pushes total costs well above 5% of income
  • Lower income: Retirees, part-time workers, or single-income households where 5% of net income is a lower absolute number
Example: When it works
Total medical costs: CHF 12,000
Net income: CHF 60,000
5% threshold: CHF 3,000

CHF 12,000 − CHF 3,000 = CHF 9,000

Result: CHF 9,000 deductible.

What Counts as a Medical Expense?

  • Yes: Dentist, pharmacy (prescriptions), physiotherapy, hospital bills, doctor visits — only amounts NOT covered by insurance
  • Yes: Prescription eyeglasses/contacts (with medical prescription)
  • No: Cosmetic procedures, wellness treatments, gym memberships, over-the-counter supplements
  • Borderline: Alternative medicine — accepted if performed by a recognized practitioner
Agent tip: Always calculate the threshold before presenting the deduction. It is much better to say "Unfortunately your medical costs of CHF 5,200 don't exceed the 5% threshold of CHF 7,800, so no deduction is available" than to claim CHF 5,200 and have the tax office reject it.
