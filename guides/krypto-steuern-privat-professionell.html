Crypto and Taxes in Zurich: Private vs. Professional Trading

22. Februar 2026Lesezeit: 5 Min.KryptowährungBitcoinVermögen

Switzerland is famously crypto-friendly — private capital gains are tax-free. But "private" has limits. Cross the line into "professional" trading and suddenly every gain is taxable income. Here are the five criteria the tax office uses to decide.

The Basic Rule

For private investors, the rule is simple:

  • Wealth tax: Declare your crypto portfolio at market value as of December 31st
  • Capital gains: TAX-FREE (no income tax on profits from buying and selling)
  • Mining/staking income: Taxable as income
The golden rule: Private crypto investors in Switzerland pay wealth tax on their holdings but zero income tax on their trading gains. This is one of the most favorable crypto tax regimes in the world.

When Does "Private" Become "Professional"?

The tax office evaluates five criteria. No single criterion is decisive — they look at the overall picture:

CriterionPrivateProfessional
Trading frequencyA few trades per monthDaily or multiple times daily
Holding periodMonths to yearsHours to days
Volume relative to incomePortfolio is small vs. salaryTrading volume dwarfs employment income
Use of leverage/marginNo borrowed fundsMargin trading, leveraged positions
Systematic approachCasual, no fixed strategyAlgorithmic, systematic, uses bots

What to Ask the Taxpayer

When a taxpayer mentions crypto, the agent should ask:

  1. What is the total market value of your crypto portfolio as of December 31st?
  2. How frequently did you trade? (Daily? Weekly? Monthly?)
  3. Did you use any leverage, margin trading, or automated trading tools?
  4. What was your approximate trading volume relative to your employment income?
If in doubt: If the taxpayer's trading activity is borderline, recommend consulting a tax advisor. The private-vs-professional line is subjective and a wrong call can result in back-taxes plus interest.

How to Declare Crypto Wealth

For the wealth declaration, use the market price on December 31st. The ESTV publishes official crypto exchange rates for major currencies (BTC, ETH, and others). Use these official rates, not the price from your exchange app — they may differ slightly.
