Don't Leave Money on the Table: Verrechnungssteuer for Beginners

5. März 2026Lesezeit: 5 Min.VerrechnungssteuerWertschriftenDividenden

Every year, thousands of Swiss taxpayers leave money on the table by forgetting to claim back the Verrechnungssteuer (anticipatory withholding tax). If you hold Swiss stocks, bonds, or even just a savings account paying interest — you are almost certainly affected.

What Is Verrechnungssteuer?

The Verrechnungssteuer is a 35% tax automatically withheld at source on Swiss investment income — dividends from Swiss companies, interest from Swiss bank accounts, and certain other investment returns. It is designed as a mechanism to ensure people declare their investment income. If you declare it properly, you get the 35% back.

The deal: Declare your investment income honestly on your Steuererklärung → get the 35% Verrechnungssteuer refunded. Hide your investment income → lose the 35% forever. The system is designed to reward honesty.

How to Claim the Refund

The process is simple but easily overlooked:

  1. Declare all Swiss dividends and interest as Vermögensertrag (investment income) on your Steuererklärung
  2. Fill in the Wertschriften- und Guthabenverzeichnis (securities and assets register) completely
  3. Tick the box requesting the Verrechnungssteuer refund
  4. The refund amount will be credited against your final tax bill

Where to Find the Withholding Amount

Your bank or broker statement will typically show:

  • Gross dividend: The full dividend amount before tax
  • Verrechnungssteuer (35%): The amount withheld
  • Net payment: What you actually received

The Verrechnungssteuer line is what you need. For a UBS securities portfolio showing CHF 8,600 in gross dividends, the Verrechnungssteuer would be CHF 3,010 (35% of CHF 8,600). That CHF 3,010 is yours to claim back.

Common mistake: Some people fill in the dividend income but forget to tick the refund box or leave the Wertschriftenverzeichnis empty. Both of these can cause the refund to be denied. The tax office needs to see that you've declared all your securities — the refund is conditional on full disclosure.

The Consistency Check

If you declare dividend income but leave the Wertschriftenverzeichnis empty, the tax office will notice the inconsistency. Dividends imply you own securities — so where are they in your wealth declaration? Always make sure your investment income and your securities register tell a consistent story.
