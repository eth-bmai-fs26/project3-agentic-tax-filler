Divorce and Taxes: The Alimony Trap Most People Fall Into

19. Februar 2026Lesezeit: 5 Min.ScheidungUnterhaltKinderabzug

Divorce changes everything about your tax filing — from your status to your deductions to how support payments are treated. And the #1 mistake? Mixing up spousal support and child support. They look similar on a bank statement, but the tax treatment is completely different.

Filing Status After Divorce

The rule is simple but the timing matters enormously:

  • If the divorce was finalized before December 31st of the tax year → file separately for the full year
  • If the divorce was finalized after December 31st → file jointly for the full year

The key date is the legal finalization of the divorce (Rechtskraft), not when you separated or when the court hearing took place.

The Critical Distinction: Spousal vs. Child Support

Payment TypeFor RecipientFor Payer
Ehegattenunterhalt (spousal support)Taxable incomeTax-deductible
Kinderunterhalt (child support)NOT incomeNOT deductible
Why this matters so much: If Yuki receives CHF 800/month in spousal support and CHF 1,500/month in child support, only the CHF 800 is taxable income. Declaring the full CHF 2,300 as income would cost her hundreds of francs in unnecessary taxes. Conversely, declaring none of it would be underreporting.

The Alimony Math Check

Always verify that actual payments match the divorce decree. If the decree says CHF 2,300/month but only CHF 1,700 arrives, this could mean:

  • The ex-partner underpaid (common)
  • Some payments were made in cash or through a different channel
  • The payments started mid-month

Flag any discrepancy. Declare what the divorce decree stipulates as the legal entitlement, but note the actual amount received if different.

Kinderabzug After Divorce

Only one parent can claim the Kinderabzug for each child. Typically, the parent with primary custody claims it. If custody is shared 50/50, the parents must agree on who claims — the tax office will reject duplicate claims.

Alleinerziehenden-Abzug (Single Parent Deduction)

A frequently missed deduction: if you are a single or divorced parent and the child lives primarily with you, you may claim the Alleinerziehenden-Abzug in Zurich. This is a cantonal deduction that reduces your taxable income. It is not available to married couples and requires that you are the primary caregiver.

Don't forget: The Alleinerziehenden-Abzug must often be actively searched for in the form — it is not always prominently displayed. Make sure it is claimed if the taxpayer qualifies.
